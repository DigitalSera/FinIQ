Financial Glossary

Simple, layman explanations of the terms we use in this app.

Revenue

The total money you collect from your customers during a sale, before deducting any costs.

Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

What you originally paid your supplier to buy the exact items you just sold. E.g. If you sold 2 bags of rice, COGS is what those 2 bags cost you.

Gross Profit

Revenue minus Cost of Goods.
This is your profit on the products themselves, before paying for transport or rent.

Net Profit

Gross Profit minus all Expenses.
Your actual "take-home" money. If this is negative, you are operating at a loss.

Profit Margin

Profit Margin shows what percentage of your sales becomes real profit after all costs have been deducted.

(Net Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100

If a business sells goods worth ₦100,000 and keeps ₦25,000 as profit after expenses, the profit margin is 25%.

Profit Leakage

Areas where money is slipping away without you noticing: Selling items below cost price, items expiring on shelves, or sudden spikes in expenses.
